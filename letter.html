A letter from the heart

Alishba, This Is For You

I am not writing this to sound perfect. I am writing this because my heart feels too full, and silence is not where I want our love to stay.

My Darling

Alishba, some people enter life quietly, but slowly become impossible to replace. You are that person for me.

You became a thought I smiled at, a name my heart softened for, and a presence that made ordinary days feel different. I still think about how everything began, the department corridor, the welcome party, those little excuses to talk, the awkward moments, the silence, the reels, the calculus help, the farewell, the picture, and then those daily conversations.

Every little thing became a part of a story that I now hold very close to my heart. When things are good between us, there is a different brightness in me. When you are close, even through words, I feel peaceful. When there is distance, even beautiful days feel incomplete.

I love the way you became a part of my life. I love even more the way you became a part of my family’s happiness. Seeing my mother and sisters know about you, like you, talk about you, and connect with you makes my heart feel full.

It feels like Allah gave me a glimpse of something I always wanted, a love that does not only stay between two people, but slowly becomes home.

Our Little Names

I love how our bond has its own little language. Alishba, Aalu, ladoo, kadoo, danedaar, bhengi aankhon wali, darling, cutie, cutiepie, tharki junior, and then that random but sweet “patootie” you call me.

Maybe these names sound silly to the world, but to me they feel like small pieces of us. Every nickname carries teasing, comfort, romance, and that private softness that only we understand.

The Romantic Truth

There is a side of my love for you that is soft, romantic, private, and only ours. It lives in the way I miss your attention, the way I crave your closeness, and the way your little words can change my whole mood.

I miss the sweetness that belongs only to us. The teasing, the warmth, the comfort, the little naughty jokes wrapped in love, and the kind of affection that feels safe because it is with you.

I do not need to say everything loudly. Some feelings are more beautiful when they stay between two hearts. Just know this, I miss being close to you emotionally, romantically, and lovingly.

Some Poetry For You

You came quietly,
but now my heart does not know how to be loud without your name in it.

The moon may brighten the sky,
but the light I look for still lives in you.

Some people become memories,
but you became home.

I looked at the world,
then looked at you,
and understood why hearts choose one person and stay there.

If love had a face in my story,
it would smile like you.

I do not need a perfect world,
I only need a peaceful us.

What I Really Want

Alishba, I do not want our love to stand behind ego. I do not want a fight to become bigger than our memories. I do not want silence to win when my heart is still calling for you.

I want us to talk calmly. I want to understand what hurt you. I want to say what is in my heart without anger. I want us to remember that we are not enemies. We are two people who love each other deeply.

I am not asking for perfection. I am asking for softness. One honest conversation. One chance to fix what feels heavy. One moment where love becomes louder than pride.

My Heart Still Chooses You

No matter how upset things feel right now, I love you deeply, value you deeply, and still want a tomorrow with you in it.
